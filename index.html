Zivvo / Overview
Off-peak yield marketplace for leisure
The thesis

Off-peak deals for going out.

EatClub applies airline-style yield management to empty restaurant tables — sell a perishable 6pm-Tuesday seat at up to 50% off rather than let it expire worthless. Zivvo applies the same logic to entertainment capacity: a bowling lane at 2pm, an escape room at 4pm, a paintball field on a quiet weekday morning.

Why entertainment is a stronger version of the model A leisure venue's capacity is even more perishable and higher-fixed-cost than a restaurant table — lanes, karts and rooms cost the same whether empty or full. Every off-peak booking is near-pure incremental margin. Bowlero (400 venues targeted by 2026) publicly cites "data-driven yield management" as a survival requirement — operators already want this tooling; nobody has built the cross-venue marketplace for it.

The one-line wedge

Be the consumer-loved, frictionless off-peak yield/demand layer that integrates with venues' existing booking systems and undercuts Groupon's brutal take — in a market growing ~26%+ a year where incumbents are already asking for exactly this.

01 · Landscape

Market & competitors

The competitive set splits cleanly into three camps: deal marketplaces operators dislike, activity OTAs built for tourists, and booking software that holds the inventory but generates zero demand. Nobody has assembled EatClub's full stack for leisure.

The blueprint: how EatClub works

  • Yield management for empty tables. Restaurants post time-limited deals — typically up to 50% off the whole bill — targeted at off-peak windows. Venues set days/times; AI recommends when and how deep to discount.
  • Frictionless redemption is the killer UX. "EatClub Pay" is a digital Mastercard in Apple/Google Wallet — tap to pay and the discount applies automatically. No codes, no vouchers, no staff training.
  • Margin-contribution framing (Marco Pierre White: "It's all about contribution to costs") — a half-price cover beats an empty seat absorbing fixed costs.
  • Consultative, sales-led onboarding. Exact commission isn't public — pricing gated behind "book a demo." Implies a negotiated success-fee and a BD-heavy supply side.

Benchmark: ~3,000–5,000 venues, 2M+ downloads, AU + UK (London May 2025), ~$27M Series B at ~$200M valuation, partnered with CommBank's 8.8M users. Claims ~10× off-peak uplift, ~12% annual revenue lift.

Where Zivvo wins

Where incumbents fail venues

• Groupon's 35–50% take + deal-hunter churn destroys margins.

• OTAs charge 20–30% and own the customer.

• Booking SaaS has inventory but no demand.

• Chains discount in silos, no aggregation or AI pricing.

Where incumbents fail consumers

• No single "what discounted fun can I do tonight?" app.

• Voucher friction: codes, print-outs, "show staff."

• Nothing personalized to who you're booking for.

Zivvo's five strategic moves
  1. Copy EatClub-Pay frictionless redemption — the defensible UX moat.
  2. Sell "margin contribution," not "discounting" — LBE's higher perishability makes this an easier pitch.
  3. Integrate, don't displace — be the demand layer on Resova/FareHarbor/Peek via their APIs.
  4. Undercut Groupon hard — a transparent take well below 35–50%.
  5. Win the clearest verticals first — escape rooms, bowling, karting/paintball, trampoline parks.
02 · Architecture

Technology stack

The decisive fact: this is an inventory + booking system, not a coupon catalog. Venues sell capacity over time. Get the booking engine right — with database-enforced conflict prevention — and the rest is conventional. Highest-leverage decision: one TypeScript stack from mobile → dashboard → API.

The booking engine — where Zivvo is won or lost

Model capacity over time. Entities: Venue → Resource → InventorySlot (a tstzrange with capacity & price) → Booking. Two safeguards prevent double-booking:

1 · Time-overlap integrity

ALTER TABLE inventory_slot
  ADD CONSTRAINT no_overlap
  EXCLUDE USING gist (
    resource_id WITH =,
    slot_range  WITH &&)
  WHERE (status <> 'closed');

The same resource can never have two overlapping bookings — the database enforces it.

2 · Capacity under concurrency

BEGIN;
SELECT reserved_capacity, total_capacity
  FROM inventory_slot
  WHERE id = $1 FOR UPDATE;
-- check reserved + party <= total
UPDATE inventory_slot SET
  reserved_capacity = reserved_capacity + $party
  WHERE id = $1;
INSERT INTO booking (...) VALUES (...);
COMMIT;

FOR UPDATE serializes concurrent buyers. Add a Redis counter for flash-sale spikes.

Cart "hold": on checkout, create Booking(status='held', expires_at=now()+10min); a BullMQ job releases expired holds. Dynamic pricing: start rule-based, materialize the final price onto each slot. Defer ML.

POS integration is the hardest non-engineering problem The entertainment-POS landscape (Semnox, Intercard, Embed; QubicaAMF, Brunswick) is fragmented and often closed. MVP ships with NO POS integration — operators manage inventory in the dashboard, redemption is in-app QR. Then an adapter pattern (FareHarbor/Bookeo/Resova first), then deep partnerships.

04 · Money

Payments & money flow

Zivvo is a marketplace, not a normal merchant: when a consumer pays $40, most belongs to the venue — you keep commission. That requires split payments, KYC'd payees, and correct liability/tax attribution. A plain processor can't do this.

Headline recommendation Build on Stripe Connect with Express accounts, using destination charges + application_fee_amount, and manual capture for deposits & group bookings. Fastest compliant, low-PCI-scope path. Evaluate Adyen only at 8-figure GMV.

Provider comparison

ProviderFee (US)Verdict
Stripe Connect Pick2.9% + $0.30; $2/mo per accountFastest launch, best docs, transparent pricing, Stripe-hosted KYC, native split.
Adyen for PlatformsInterchange++; ~€1,000/mo minBest rate at high volume + EU acquiring. Phase-2 migration.

Account type → Express (Stripe-hosted branded KYC). Charge type → destination charges for single-venue; separate charges + transfers for bundles / redemption-gated payouts.

destination = venue, application_fee_amount = $7.20.
STEP 3
Stripe fee ~$1.46
Platform absorbs it → Zivvo nets ~$5.74.
STEP 4
Venue gets $32.80

STEP 5
Payout
Daily/weekly with ~2-day buffer, or redemption-gated.

Refunds: reverse fee & transfer so the venue's share unwinds. Disputes: platform is liable for uncovered negatives — mitigate with payout buffers, redemption-gated payouts, Radar, 3DS/SCA, on_behalf_of.

Recommended commission model

Core: 18% venue commission

Well below Groupon's 30–50%, in line with Fever. Anchor launch venues at 15%.

+ ~5% consumer service fee

Transparent at checkout. Boosts margin without touching the venue's take.

Future SaaS tier

$0 / $49 / $99/mo that reduces commission (18% → 12%) for whales.

Avoid at launch

Listing fees deter sign-ups with no demand yet. Add promoted placement later.

Methods, deposits & compliance

Payment methods

Cards + Apple/Google Pay, saved cards, Klarna/Afterpay above ~$150 (parties), internal wallet for gift cards.

Deposits & no-shows

Manual capture holds funds (7–30 days). Parties: 25–30% deposit + pre-auth. Partial capture for no-show fees.

Compliance

Stripe Elements → SAQ A PCI. Stripe-hosted KYC/AML, Radar fraud, auto 3DS/SCA, Stripe Tax.

05 · Plan

MVP & roadmap

Ship a tight, defensible v1 that proves the core loop: operator lists off-peak slots → consumer discovers & books → redeems → both see value.

PHASE 0 · MVP

Consumer

Onboarding + activity quiz · feed, browse, map · "Tonight"/"Weekend" rails · filters (party size, age) · venue profiles · slot booking + waivers · party flow · QR redemption · favorites · reviews · push/SMS · points + referrals

Operator + Infra

Onboarding · inventory/capacity · deal creator + auto-discounts · package builder · calendar + QR check-in · analytics · payouts. Tech: single ECS + RDS Postgres (EXCLUDE + FOR UPDATE) + Redis holds · Stripe Connect · Algolia · no POS, rule-based pricing.

PHASE 1 · Engagement & liquidity

Real-time pushes ("3 slots left") · lifecycle messaging · referral & group-invite virality · richer analytics · first POS adapters · split-the-bill, RSVP, gamified passport.

PHASE 2 · Scale & moat

Extract booking into a Go service if hot · ML dynamic pricing · deep POS integrations (Semnox/Embed/Qubica) · Elasticsearch · evaluate Adyen · venue SaaS tier · multi-region.

The one thing to over-invest in early The time-slot/capacity booking engine with database-enforced conflict prevention. Everything else is replaceable — a marketplace that double-books venues is not.
